Walking Through Marrakech

The streets folded endlessly into one another. Dust rose from scooters weaving through the crowds while the late afternoon light reflected off the faded pink walls.

I wandered most of the day carrying my camera without much direction. Some neighborhoods felt calm and residential while others exploded into noise and movement without warning.

The best moments usually happened after getting lost. Small doorways, rooftop cafes, hidden courtyards, and conversations that would never have happened if I followed a map too carefully.

By evening the city became quieter. The streets cooled down and the entire atmosphere softened into something slower and more reflective.

May 14, 2026

Rain Along the Coast of Maine

The weather changed every twenty minutes. Fog rolled in across the harbor before clearing just long enough for sunlight to hit the water again.

Most of the afternoon was spent walking along wet rocks and watching lobster boats disappear into the mist.

Maine has a strange ability to feel both isolated and familiar at the same time. Even empty places seem full of history.

Everything felt quieter than usual. The fog blurred the coastline until the ocean and sky almost disappeared into one another.
